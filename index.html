Journal Entry

The Longing is about the sorrow in His love—not the kind that’s angry or impatient, but the kind that aches with compassion. He showed me that His mercy is not just for saving me from something, it’s for bringing me into something deeper. A relationship. His love is not a distant light—it’s a closeness that mourns when we suffer, and celebrates when we return.

Journal: Behind the Song

There’s a line in this song that means more to me than I can explain:
“Your love is the love I know in this world.”

That’s not just a beautiful sentiment. It’s a deep reality.
The way we’re loved—especially early in life—becomes our blueprint for what love is. Whether that love is kind or cruel, present or absent, healing or harmful… it becomes the definition. Until it is challenged and changed by a greater, more patient example.

A child doesn’t know to question it. They just receive it, absorb it, and carry it forward. It becomes the lens through which every future act of love is measured. So when I sing that line, I’m speaking to something sacred and sobering:

“Your love is the love I know in this world.”

It’s what taught me what love is, for better or worse. It shaped how I receive love, how I give it, and what I expect from it. It’s what I’ll carry into eternity as my lived understanding of love.

This could be between lovers, friends, family. It could be the love that saved you… or the love that wounded you first. Either way, it leaves a mark. And that’s what this song is reaching toward—acknowledging that deep truth, that we teach each other what this powerful, invisible force is by how we love.
